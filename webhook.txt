“Webhooks are nothing but a way for one app to communicate with another.”

WHAT ARE THEY?
Let’s take a simple example to understand why we even need Webhooks.

Imagine you have connected your app with Stripe (a payment service). You want your app to be notified every time a user makes a payment. How do we do this?

We have two options:
Keep checking Stripe repeatedly (Polling) → Inefficient
Let Stripe notify us when a payment happens (Webhook) → Efficient

But Wait… Isn’t This the Same as SSE?
You might be wondering:

“Webhooks send updates. But doesn’t SSE (Server-Sent Events) do the same thing? What’s the difference?”

Let’s clarify that before moving ahead.

SSE vs. Webhooks
Both Webhooks and SSE send updates, but their use cases are very different.

SSE (Server-Sent Events): Used when a server needs to send updates to a client (browser).
Webhooks: Used when one server needs to send updates to another server.
Example of SSE:
Let’s say you’re watching a stock market dashboard that updates in real-time. Your browser connects to the server, and the server keeps pushing stock price updates.
  -Stripe has a server, and your app is another server.
  -SSE only works for server-to-browser updates.
  -We need server-to-server communication → Webhook is the solution.

How We Used Webhooks for WhatsApp Integration
At our company, we had to integrate our chatbot with WhatsApp because some businesses handle a large portion of their customer queries on WhatsApp.

The Use Case:
Whenever a customer messages a business on WhatsApp, our bot should automatically reply.

Step-by-Step WhatsApp Webhook Flow:
-User sends a message to the business’s WhatsApp number.
-WhatsApp (Meta) triggers a webhook → Calls our webhook URL with the message details.
-Our backend processes the message (retrieves response from the chatbot).
-We call the WhatsApp Graph API to send the response back to the user.
-User receives a reply from the bot on WhatsApp.

Link showing flow : https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:720/format:webp/1*_kPIEpLaeNbgFEL4sixSSw.png
You might wonder:

“Why can’t we just directly send a message to the user?”
Because WhatsApp doesn’t allow businesses to send direct messages to users for spam prevention.

WhatsApp’s Rules:
-You can’t message users first (they must initiate the chat).
-You must use the WhatsApp Graph API to reply.
-If 24 hours pass without a response, you can only send pre-approved template messages.

Webhooks are super powerful because they let apps talk to each other instantly without constant polling.
